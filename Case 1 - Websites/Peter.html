Information for Patients Considering Participation

This study looks at how cancer treatment may affect the heart and lungs. Many patients feel well after chemoradiotherapy—but subtle changes may occur that we can monitor and understand better.

Why this study?

  • To learn whether chemoradiotherapy can cause pulmonary hypertension (PH).
  • To improve long-term care and reduce future risks for patients.
  • To detect cardiopulmonary changes earlier and more accurately.

What participation involves

  • Standard heart ultrasound (echocardiography) at 0, 6, 12, 26, and 52 weeks.
  • Optional cardiac MRI at baseline, 12 weeks, and 1 year.
  • No invasive procedures — no catheterization.
  • Completion of quality-of-life questionnaires.

Eligibility

  • Lung or esophageal cancer treated with chemoradiotherapy.
  • WHO performance status 0–2.
  • No previous thoracic radiotherapy.

Information for Clinicians & Participating Centers

Overview

  • Prospective multicenter cohort study.
  • Primary endpoint: Echo-assessed high-risk PH at 1 year.
  • Secondary endpoints include MRI parameters, QoL, biomarkers.

Operational Requirements

  • Local echocardiography at 5 follow-up moments.
  • Optional cMRI, required when echo suggests PH.
  • Submit RT plans & dose distributions.
  • Follow standardized toxicity and PROM documentation.
